Dual-Time Representation Framework for Interactive Music Transcription

Abstract

We present a framework for interactive music transcription (IMT) that enables users to efficiently correct outputs of automatic music transcription (AMT) methods. While existing IMT systems typically use physical time (PT) representations (measured in seconds), which can precisely describe note timings, metrical time (MT) representations (measured in fractions of a beat) are more suitable for score typesetting and can reduce the user’s cognitive cost in understanding musical structure. To enable corrections in MT-based note sequences (MTNSs) derived from PT-based note sequences (PTNSs) produced by AMT methods, and to support coherent and flexible operations across both time domains, we propose data structures consisting of beat annotations, a MTNS, and a PTNS. Moreover, to facilitate user interaction, we develop three core processing methods: rhythm quantization (PTNS to MTNS), onset/offset time alignment (MTNS to PTNS), and candidate generation for note corrections. These methods are formulated within a unified generative model that incorporates statistical knowledge of musical scores and performances. Taking singing transcription as a concrete example, we implemented an IMT system based on the proposed framework, and experimental results demonstrated its potential in improving the human correction process.
